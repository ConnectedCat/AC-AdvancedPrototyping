Using a battery with a Particle Photon board.

Parts you'll need:

OR if you don't mind a bit of soldering

You'll need this to connect your battery to the breadboard

Batteries come in different sizes and power capacities. Look at the mAh rating - the greater the number the longer the battery is going to last and the larger the battery itself is going to be. Make sure the voltage in 3.3 - 3.8. Above 4V can already be damaging to a Particle board.

You'll need a carger for your battery. Depending on the battery size you might want to get a faster charger

Pins you'll use:

The ground connection can be common with other elements. The power connection should be kept separate and connected directly to the pin.
